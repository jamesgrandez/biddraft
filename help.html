Field Guide

What Each Field Means

Walk through every field in the submission form. Required fields are marked — optional fields improve your bid but won't block submission.

01
Your Name
Required
Your first and last name. This appears on the bid as the contractor contact. Use your real name — not your company name (that's the next field).
02
Company Name
Required
Your licensed business name exactly as it appears on your Broward County or Florida DBPR license. This goes on the bid header — it needs to match your license.
03
License Number
Required Improves Bid
Your Florida state license number. Trade-specific formats:

• Roofing: CCC followed by 7 digits (e.g. CCC1234567)
• HVAC/AC: CAC followed by 6 digits (e.g. CAC123456)
• Electrical: EC or ER followed by 13 digits
• Plumbing: CFC followed by 7 digits
• General Contractor: CGC or CBC followed by 7 digits

Including this makes your bid look legitimate and professional to clients and adjusters.
04
Email Address
Required
The email address we send your completed bid draft to. Double-check this — a typo here means your bid goes nowhere.
05
Phone Number
Optional
We'll only use this if we have a quick follow-up question about your scope. Speeds up delivery if your description needs clarification.
06
Trade Type
Required
Select your trade from the dropdown. This tells BidDraft which permit category, code sections, and warranty language to use. If your job crosses trades (e.g. a GC managing both framing and electrical), select General Contractor and describe all trades in the scope of work field.
07
Property Address
Required Affects Permit Fees
The full address of the job site — not your business address. Why this matters: permit fees and requirements differ between unincorporated Broward County and municipalities like City of Sunrise, Plantation, Tamarac, or Davie. Getting the jurisdiction right gets the permit fee right.
08
Job Type
Required
Select the closest match: New Installation, Full Replacement, Repair/Patch, Addition, or Remodel/Renovation. This affects the permit type and scope language significantly. A "full replacement" triggers tear-off and disposal line items that a "repair" doesn't.
09
Scope of Work Description
Required Most Important Field
This is the most important field. The more detail you give here, the better your bid draft. Write it the way you'd explain the job to a customer — what are you doing, where, how big, with what materials?

Include: Square footage / linear feet / system size · Existing conditions (is there something being removed or replaced?) · Any specific requirements the customer gave you · Anything unusual about the job.

See the Good vs. Bad examples section below for each trade.
// Good example (roofing) "Replace existing 2,200 sq ft asphalt shingle roof on single-family home in Sunrise FL 33323. Remove existing two layers of shingles and underlayment, inspect decking, replace any damaged sheets, install new 30 lb felt, install OC Duration architectural shingles in Onyx Black. Replace 6 turtle vents with mushroom vents. Need Broward County permit and wind mitigation report for insurance."
10
Materials / Brands
Optional Improves Accuracy
If you already know what materials or brands you're using, list them here. Examples: "Carrier 3-ton Infinity series", "OC Duration shingles", "Trex Enhance composite decking", "Moen Posi-Temp shower valve." Brand names let us include the correct product specs and NOA numbers where applicable.
11
Your Labor Rate
Optional Improves Pricing
If you leave this blank, we use South Florida market averages for your trade. If your rates are different — higher or lower — enter your rate here and we'll build the labor line items around your actual numbers. Format: "$85/hr" or "$12/sq for install" or "lump sum $4,500 labor."
12
Estimated Job Size
Required
A rough estimate of the total job value. This helps calibrate the bid's overall pricing and detail level. If you're unsure, pick the closest range. It doesn't need to be exact.
13
When Does This Bid Need to Be Sent?
Optional
If you have a deadline — a customer meeting, an insurance appointment, a submission deadline — tell us. We prioritize urgent bids. Standard delivery is within 24 hours. Same-day delivery available for submissions received before 3pm EST.

Common Questions

Quick Answers

How long does it take to get my bid draft back? ▸
Standard delivery is within 24 hours of your submission. Submissions received before 3pm EST typically get same-day delivery. If you have an urgent deadline, note it in the "When does this need to be sent?" field and we'll prioritize your bid.
What if I don't know all the details yet? ▸
Submit what you have. If your scope is missing a critical detail — like square footage or system size — we'll email you back with one specific question before generating. We'd rather ask than guess and get the numbers wrong. We'll still get your bid back same-day in most cases.
Will the pricing in the bid match my actual costs? ▸
The bid uses current South Florida market averages for your trade. If your actual costs are different — higher materials costs, different labor rate — you can adjust the numbers before sending to your customer. Think of the draft as a professionally structured starting point, not a final number. That's why we include the line item breakdown — so you can see and change each individual figure.
What do I do with the bid draft once I receive it? ▸
Review it, adjust any numbers that don't match your actual costs, add your company letterhead or logo, and send it to your customer. The bid is yours completely — you own it, edit it, and send it as your own professional proposal. BidDraft AI generates the structure and language; you verify the numbers and put your name on it.
Are the Broward County permit fees accurate? ▸
We use current Broward County published fee schedules. Permit fees are shown as estimates in the bid because the exact fee depends on the declared job value at the time of permit application. The ranges we include are accurate for typical jobs in each trade — within 5–10% of the actual fee in most cases. Always confirm the final permit fee with Broward County Building Division before submitting your permit application.
Can I submit multiple jobs at once? ▸
Yes — submit each job as a separate form submission. Each one gets its own bid draft delivered to your email. If you're on the Founding Member plan, all your submissions are prioritized and you can submit as many jobs per month as you need.
